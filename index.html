Frequently asked questions to your contractors

Always verify that your contractor holds a valid, active license and carries both general liability and workers' compensation insurance to protect your property and investment.
A reputable contractor should be happy to share references and examples of previous work similar to your project.
Experience matters. Ask about the contractor's track record, years in business, and specific expertise relevant to your project type.
Understanding a contractor's specialization helps ensure they have the right skills and equipment for your specific needs.
Request a detailed, written breakdown of costs including materials, labor, permits, and any potential additional expenses before work begins.
Discuss the change-order process upfront so you know how unexpected costs and scope changes will be communicated and approved.
Clarify whether the contractor or homeowner is responsible for pulling required permits and scheduling inspections with local authorities.
Ask about workmanship warranties, manufacturer warranties on materials, and what the process is for addressing issues after project completion.
Confirm that daily cleanup and final debris removal are included in the estimate, and understand how waste disposal will be handled.
Obtain a clear estimate of how long the remodeling project is expected to take from start to finish, keeping in mind potential delays and factors that may impact the timeline.
